Meeting Minutes, July 1999

Information Technology Executive Council
(ITEC)
Tuesday, July 13, 1999
State Capitol, Room 423-S

The meeting of the Information Technology Executive Council was called to order at 1:30 p.m. by Dan Stanley, Chair. 

Executive Summary: 
 This was the second meeting of the Information Technology Executive Council (ITEC).  Introductions and greetings were exchanged.  Secretary Dan Stanley, Chair, presented plaques to the graduates of the first round of the Project Management training.  Fred Boesch, Legislative CITO presented proposed changes to three current ITEC policies.  Conditional approval was given for these changes.  Don Heiman gave a report on State IT Resources and Y2K status.  Approval was given to the Moratorium on Changes to Applications.  Dave Merrill briefed the Council on Release 4.0 Kansas IT Architecture and discussed the Kansas Information Technology Architecture Review Board.  Richard Bendis of KTEC discussed planning initiatives and was given approval to form a Kansas Information Technology Action Committee. 

Greetings and Introductions
Chair Stanley opened the meeting by welcoming the members along with graduates of the first Project Management Course.  Certificates were presented to the graduates and congratulated by Chair Stanley.

Policy Change Recommendations
Fred Boesch, CITO for the Legislature introduced some changes to policies 1200, 2500 and 2510 for the Council's consideration. 

The first change discussed was in policy 1200 regarding acceptable use of the Internet.  In section 6.3 Fred suggested that the last sentence be deleted.  This pertained to written policies and procedures for Internet activity and cost recovery. 

In policy 2500, Fred proposed including additional verbiage in sections 4.2 and 4.3.   Section 7.2 was enhanced and 7.3 adds an attachment to the policy regarding the review of active projects by the JCIT.  These sections refer to matrixes that are not currently being used, but would impact current projects. 

Fred added the two statutory references to K.S.A. 75-7211 to this policy as sections 4.2 and 4.3.  Sections 6.6 and 6.7 were added because they are required by law.  Additions to sections 7.4 and 7.5 pertain to oversight reports for active projects and their submission to the JCIT.  This is a current practice, so isn't really a change. 

There was discussion about the interpretation of K.S.A. 75-7211, which is referenced in policies 2500 and 2510 as sections 4.2 and 4.3.  There was further discussion and a recommended motion by Fred Boesch that approval without section 4.2 in policies 2500 and 2510.  Secretary Chronister moved that the policy changes be accepted with the exception of section 4.2 in policies 2500 and 2510 and asked for further clarification at the next meeting.  The policies should be renumbered accordingly.  Duane Goossen seconded the motion.  Motion passed.

CITA Report
Overview State IT Resources    
 Don Heiman gave an overview of IT in Kansas.  He presented information as to IT budget amounts, geographic information, ITEC and Department of Administration organizational charts.  He discussed the organizational structure of DISC and what services they provide throughout the state.  In regard to the Kansas IT infrastructure, he discussed the responsibilities of state agencies.  Don provided information on the shared data centers provided by DISC along with discussing what ITEC and Senate Bill 5 mean to Kansas government. 

Don also introduced the employees of the Kansas Information Technology Office; John Oliver, Max Williams, Kelly Brainard and Phil Wallack. 

Y2K Briefing - Moratorium on Changes to Applications  
Don briefed the Council on the status of Y2K remediation efforts as of July 9, 1999.  Don provided handouts with charting of the percentages of time left for repair for Mission Critical 1, 2 and 3 applications and hardware.  Year 2000 status information can be found at http://y2k.state.ks.us/agystatus or http://y2k.state.ks.us/agystatus/servicestatus.htm .  In addition to this information Don noted that 93.85% of Federal interfaces are compliant and state ready.  

Also presented was information on Moratorium on Changes to Applications.  This is a proposal for a graduated freeze on changes to application code and infrastructure upgrades.  The proposal is modeled from the Federal Reserves' program for mitigating century date change related risks. 

This proposal asks agency heads and IT directors for final decisions on IT changes based on the agency's change control procedure.  Agencies must file their procedure with the Kansas Information Technology Office (KITO) and approvals should be logged.  

Agencies may file their own moratorium plan, but if they don't, then the presented moratorium should be followed. 

Secretary Chronister moved to accept the policy.  Mr. George Fuciu seconded the motion.  Motion passed.

Release 4.0 Kansas IT Architecture/Kansas Information Technology Architecture Review Board (K-TARB)  
Dave Merrill presented information on the Kansas Information Technology Architecture Review Board (K-TARB) and fielded questions.  

The K-TARB was created from the ITAB to promote Kansas Statewide Technical Architecture (KSTA) from the current synthetic state into an organic architecture.  Training has been completed for the chairman and co-chairman on the concepts, principles and approach to architecture development and maintenance.  Architecture sub-committees have been organized including members from Regents, DISC, and large and small state agencies.  A detailed schedule is in place for sub-architecture development and improvements to Kansas Statewide Technical Architecture (KSTA). 

Dave reviewed the design approach, architecture qualities, and lifecycle of standards and KSTA sub-architectures.  He also discussed the consolidation and future of KSTA. 

A copy of the draft version of release 4.0 was distributed to members.  It is still undergoing legal review.  For this reason it should not be released to vendors.

KTEC Planning Initiatives 
Richard Bendis with KTEC presented information to the Council seeking approval to form a Kansas Information Technology Action Committee.  Handouts were provided to all with detailed information about KTEC.  He discussed the communications revolution that is going on now and the history of change in communication as it relates to cost and technology.  He briefed the Council with statistics on industry and the economy in Kansas and what is needed to build an innovation economy. 

The mission of the proposed Committee is to develop a long-term state strategic plan, which provides a virtual state infrastructure for business, government, and academia to have full and equal access to operate efficiently, cost effectively and competitively in the global economy.  Mr. Bendis handed out lists of suggested members to serve on the task force.  

Fred commented that he would like to see input from smaller firms and businesses and that it is critical for them to be involved with this committee.  

Chair Stanley recommended that they use the SIM plan as a model for this committee and work on modifications to it.  He would like to see the new committee brief ITEC, but also take independent recommendations to the legislature.  The Action Committee plans that impact state agencies will be approved by ITEC for inclusion in the SIM plan. 

Secretary Chronister moved to adopt Chair Stanley's recommendation.  Secretary Pierce seconded the motion.  Motion passed.

Discussion/Closing Comments  
Dave Larrick inquired as to whether any further action needed to be taken to approve the policies that were given interim approval at the last meeting.  Chair Stanley said that the Council would resolve the 4.2 issue and put the policies in place at the next meeting. 

The next meeting is scheduled for October.  Tracy will e-mail and/or call members to check their schedules.  Mr. Larrick moved to adjourn.  All members seconded the motion.  Motion carried.  

The meeting adjourned at 4:00 p.m.
