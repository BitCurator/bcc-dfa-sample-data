Meeting Minutes, April 1999

Information Technology Executive Council
(ITEC)
Monday, April 12, 1999
Nickell Memorial Armory
Conference Room #1

The meeting of the Information Technology Executive Council was called to order at 1:00 p.m. by Dan Stanley, Chair.

Executive Summary:
This was the first meeting of the Information Technology Executive Council (ITEC). Introductions and greetings were exchanged. Secretary Dan Stanley, Chair, and Don Heiman, CITO for the Executive Branch briefed the council on the many different responsibilities, policies, and documents that the Council will need to familiarize themselves with. The Council approved the Mission and Responsibilities statement. Conditional, interim approval was also given for policies recommended by Don Heiman. The Council was also briefed on the State of Kansas Y2K status.

Greetings and Introductions

Chair Stanley opened the meeting by welcoming the members to the group and had everyone introduce themselves.

Overview IT Governance

Chair Stanley gave a summary of Senate Bill 5 which realigned the structure of the Information Technology administration. The Bill created Chief Information Technology Officers (CITO) for each of the three branches of government in addition to maintaining a Chief Information Technology Architect (CITA). It also created and established the ITEC and empowers the Council with planning oversight responsibility for all IT projects with an estimated cost of $250,000 or more. Approval of these projects and reporting press is the responsibility of branch CITO's.

Chair Stanley also reviewed a chart of Kansas Information Technology Governance under Senate Bill 5.

Don Heiman discussed the duties of the positions created by statute, and the reporting and procedures that were created with the legislation. He also discussed the IT Administrative Structure. Chair Stanley briefed the Council on State expenditures, staffing levels, and funding levels.

Page 16 in the notebook outlines the mission statement and objectives of the ITEC. These were reviewed. Secretary Chronister moved that the mission statement and objectives be approved. Fred Boesch seconded the motion. Motion carried.

ITEC Policies

As a result of the changes introduced in Senate Bill 5, a review and revision of the Kansas Information Resources Council (KIRC) policies was undertaken. Don Heiman briefed the Council on the KIRC policies that have been in place, and whether they should be reissued, updated, or replaced. This begins on page 25 of the notebook. A description of each policy was provided.

Mr. Heiman recommended that all policies be reissued with the exception of Policy 1100 and 2400. These policies relate to the management and review of IT Projects. It was recommended these policies be updated and replaced by several new policies outlined on pages 26 and 27.

Mr. Heiman asked for the approval of the Council to conditionally approve the replacement policies.

Mr. Wine moved that conditional Interim approval be given to draft these policies. Mr. Larrick seconded the motion. Motion carried.

ITEC Strategic Planning Documents

Don Heiman briefed the Council on the Strategic Information Management Plan. This is a long-range 5-10 year plan that was adopted last year. The intent of this plan is to provide the States IT vision and long range plan. Mr. Heiman discussed the flow of IT Architecture and how the SIM Plan ties into the annual plan.

Mr. Heiman also made note of some of the national state rankings held by the State of Kansas in several categories for Y2K compliance.

CITO Tactical Implementation Documents

Don Heiman gave an overview of the Project Management Methodology Manual prepared by the Kansas Information Technology Office. This office has adapted a methodology that had been recently developed by the State of California and was determined to be the most suitable for the State of Kansas.

The Division of Personnel Services is now working with the Kansas Information Technology Office to implement a training program to educate agency project managers in the new methodology. This three-week training program started April 5. It is Mr. Heiman's estimate that approximately 30 project managers will be trained over the next 6 months. When the training is completed, they will be eligible for an 11% project bonus based on their base salary and the successful completion of project milestones.

Y2K Briefing

Don Heiman gave a brief description of the information that could be found on the Internet about the State of Kansas Agency status for Y2K. The address is http://y2k.state.ks.us/agystatus/default.htm. The status of a number of different categories is broken down and charted.

Information was also given on how the State grades Y2K performance. The national average for state Y2K compliance of mission critical systems is 78%. Kansas has 82% of their critical systems Y2K compliant.

Discussion/Closing Comments

The next meeting is scheduled for July. Tracy will e-mail and/or call members to check their schedules. Secretary Chronister moved to adjourn. Mr. Chandler seconded the motion. Motion carried.

The meeting adjourned at 3:00 p.m.
