EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 99-2

REQUIRING EXECUTIVE BRANCH AGENCIES TO PROVIDE
THE STATE'S CHIEF INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY OFFICER
YEAR 2000 REPORTS PURSUANT TO CITO REPORTING STANDARDS

WHEREAS, information is an asset and resource for delivery of services to Kansas citizens. The State of Kansas relies upon its computer systems to manage information and accomplish its business functions with accuracy and speed. The State's information technology resources are critical to the State's ability to provide services to its citizens.

WHEREAS, many of the State's systems rely upon accurate date calculations to perform required functions. Kansas, along with the rest of the world, faces a significant problem with computer system software that will not function properly after December 31, 1999, because the technology will not recognize the century change.

WHEREAS, without a way to recognize the century change, information in the State's computer, telecommunications, and embedded chip systems will be interpreted inconsistently and date calculations will be performed incorrectly, presenting a threat to the State's ability to conduct business and serve citizens.

WHEREAS, the Kansas Information Technology Executive Council has established a goal that state agencies will have identified and corrected all computer programs that will not function properly when the Year 2000 is reached.

WHEREAS, time is of the essence if the State is to take advantage of this opportunity to both prepare for the century change and update essential information systems.

NOW THEREFORE, pursuant to the authority vested in me as Governor and chief executive of the State of Kansas by Article 1, Section 4 of the Constitution of the State of Kansas, I hereby order that each executive branch agency repair their information technology systems for Year 2000 compliance and provide Year 2000 reports to the Executive Branch Chief Information Technology Officer (CITO). Repairs shall be done in accord with schedules developed by the Chief Information Technology Officer (CITO) to insure all executive branch systems properly function in the year 2000 and beyond. Reports shall contain information required by the CITO reporting standards as set forth in the Year 2000 Web Database Asset Tracking and Readiness System, available through the Department of Administration, Division of Information systems and Communications or at the web site, http://y2k.state.ks.us.

This document shall be filed with the Secretary of State as Executive Order No. 99-2, and shall become effective immediately.

THE GOVERNOR'S OFFICE

BY THE GOVERNOR BILL GRAVES

2/12/99
