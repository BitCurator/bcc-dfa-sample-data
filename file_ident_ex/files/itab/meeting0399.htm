Meeting Minutes, March 1999
 

ITAB MEETING
Wednesday, March 17, 1999
Landon State Office Building
Room 106
1:30 - 3:30 PM

Cito Briefings and Introductions

Don reported agency IT budgets are going through the legislature smoothly. There has been a lot of support from JCIT.

There have been six or seven project plans that have come in since December 1st and they have all been filed with Division of Budget and Legislative Research.

Don gave the names of all the people who had been appointed to ITEC. They are:

Secretary Dan Stanley - Chair
Director of Budget, Duane Goossen
Chief Information Officers for all three branches
Judicial Administrator - Howard Schwartz
Regents Executive Director - Tom Bryant
Commissioner of Education - Andy Tompkins
Network Manager of INK - Debra Luling
Cabinet Secretary for Department of Revenue - Karla Pierce
Cabinet Secretary for SRS - Rochelle Chronister
Rep. for non-cabinet agencies - John Wine
Representatives from the Cities - David Larrick
Rep. for Counties - Pam Madl
Private Sector:   CIO for Western Resources - Jo Hunt
CIO for Sprint - George Fuciu
CEO for Intrust Bank - Charles Chandler IV

The private Sector appointments are for 18 months. ITEC will meet quarterly.

Don handed out a copy of Project Management Standards. It is available on the website. It is the textbook that will teach project management principles for those individuals assigned by agencies to manage $250,000+ projects. DPS has hired an instructor who will teach 120 contact hours. An exam will be given and if the student passes they will be issued a state project management certificate.

Bill McGlasson has agreed to turn on overtime for all IT people. The overtime will be turned on for one year, until March 31, 2000.

Don handed out IT Project Management Methodology training sign up sheets. There will be three sessions. The first session starts in April.

Don talked about what happened with the press concerning Y2K. When Don was in the senate ways and means committee, Sen. Salisbury asked for the report on state agencies. A copy was given to the Associated press and they noticed there were 30 applications graded with an F. Associated press published a report of the average of each agency. In the United States, states are 78% complete on Mission Critical Applications. Kansas is 73% complete with our mission critical applications. May is the targeted quarter for the press. Don wants our Mission Critical Applications score in the 90% range or higher by May 1st. The next hurdle is Business Contingency Planning. Within the next four weeks, there will be a planning guide for Business Contingency Planning. It will be sent to you. Don requested agencies file their Business Contingency Plans by July 1st. The plans will be sent to Emergency Management.

Don reported we have expended 160,000 hours on Y2K repair work, there is about 14,000 hours left.

Y2K Update

John Oliver talked about a Risk Management Report, it will be for Don and the cabinet, and it will hopefully get the high level support that is necessary. In the report, John’s group will rank the states assets in terms of criticality. Along with the risk management report there will be a CITO Risk Assessment on those assets. It is based on the CITO points.

Norton 2000 software is available on State contract. It costs $32.00 per PC and is manufactured by Symmantic Corporation. The maintenance is $10.00 a year.

Project Reporting Standards:

The quarterly project status reporting is now on the KITO website. John encouraged agencies to make sure their quarterly project reports are complete and accurate.

John handed out guidelines for the quarterly project reporting and/or bimonthly project reporting. Reporting must contain enough detail so John can report project status for JCIT. The next report is due April 10th. John will e-mail it to agencies before the report is published.

Internet Advisory Committee Report

Glenn Yancey talked about the work of the Internet task force and handed out a web primer which was finalized in December. The primer outlines best practices for Internet Site Development and Management. He also passed out ITASK Internet/Intranet Development survey. Please fill those out and send to Duncan Friend at DISC (900 SW Jackson, Room 751, Topeka, Kansas).

Deb Luling discussed the INK website. INK will help small agencies build their own website.

Kansas Technical Architecture

Dave Merrill discussed the Kansas Technical Architecture Version 3.10. There will be six different ITAB architecture committees. Don would like ITAB members to e-mail to Phil Wallack recommendations for committee membership. At the next meeting, Don would like to finalize the committees. After the committees are formed, Don wants to create a Steering Committee of ITAB membership to maintain the architecture.
