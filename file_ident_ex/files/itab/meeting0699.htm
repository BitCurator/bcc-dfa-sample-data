Meeting Minutes, June1999
 

ITAB MEETING

CITO Briefing

Don discussed the State of Kansas Y2K website and went over the handout provided.  Don receives numerous phone calls from television stations and print media about the State agency Y2K status.

Don reviewed the Kansas State Agency Y2K Status report.  He discussed mission critical applications, where we stand and the amount of hours spent on repair.  He wants all mission critical 1 applications to be at 95% or better by July 1st.  Today, mission critical 2 applications are at 87% percent and mission critical 3 applications are at 84% complete.  Any application in a hold status should be reported in another category.  We should not have equipment “on-hold.”  “If you have an asset that you are not going to cross the millennium with and it is listed as a mission critical 1, 2, or 3, lets get it off the books.”  As of July 1st, if a project is 30 days behind schedule it will drop one letter grade on the report.

Don discussed the health check audits.  He is very encouraged by the reports. 

The risk period for Y2K is tomorrow, July 1st, it is the fiscal year boundary.  The Topeka Capital Journal will be in our computer room on June 30th, to see if there are any problems.  We will start STARS batch processing at noon July 1st.  If agencies experience any problems on July 1st, please call Don at 296-3463 or John Oliver at 296-5260.

Don was pleased with how well the Technology Conference went.  KTEC has a very strong interest with technology and how it affects the Kansas economy.  KTEC will be briefing ITEC on their statewide initiatives. 

There is an ITEC meeting scheduled for July 13th, Rm. 423S of the statehouse.  ITEC will receive a copy of the Enterprise Architecture.  The Architecture plan is still in draft pending a purchasing and legal review.  Dave Merrill will be at the ITEC meeting and will do the briefing. 

JCIT will meet on July 7th.  The first round of meetings with the JCIT went well.  Don has received about 7 or 8 project plans.  All project plans were sent to the Division of the Budget, Legislative Research, and JCIT.  If you have project plans that you will be staging in July, they will go before JCIT in August.  The 3 year IT plans are due in July. 

We have successfully trained 15 individuals on IT Project Management.  ITEC will issue a certificate to those who have completed the training.  People who have gone through the project management training and manage projects are eligible for project management bonuses up to 10% of their salary.  There will also be Executive training on Project Management.  It will be 4 hours and is being scheduled for the last week of July. 

Year 2000 Update

John Oliver discussed the quarterly project status report.  New reporting categories have been added.  The report will be issued bimonthly for $1 million and projects instead of quarterly.  The report format was well received by JCIT. 

John went over the project initiation guidelines and the feasibility study report guidelines.  They are out on the web and the Division of the Budget has reviewed them and agreed to accept them as a substitute for the 518 and 519 forms.  Please review them and if there are any problems let John know. 

ITAB Committee Report

Deb Luling discussed ITASK.  ITASK will complete their work in July and join the Internet Architecture committee.

Sue Swartzman talked about workflow.  She handed out the final draft of the State of Kansas Guide to Information Technology Investment.  It should be completed by July 15th.  The committee meets monthly on the second Thursday.

Joe Hennes discussed the status of mainframe Y2K.  The repair is almost complete.  There is a meeting of the mainframe users group every Tuesday afternoon. 

Dan Swearingen went over the Disaster Recovery Business Contingency Planning.  There are monthly meetings of the Business Contingency subcommittee. 

Don went over the steps that will happen on December 31, January 1, 2000, as we clear the state.  There will be a constant readiness center located on the 8th floor of the Landon State Office Building.  Technologists can come in and clear their infrastructure products, visibly in front of the press.  We will take the Landon State Office Building and start it up on diesel backup to prove to the media that we can run the building and the mainframe data center under our diesel power.  On December 31st, at 10 p.m., we will have 60 technologists on site until 3 a.m. on January 1st.  Technologists will be available to help anyone that needs help on January 2 –3, 2000.  

Kansas Technical Architecture

Phil Wallack discussed the Enterprise Architecture preliminary draft version 4.0.  Phil reorganized the report. 

A list of the members of the review board appears at the end of the Technical Architecture. 

CJIS Security Architecture

Ron Rohrer and Norma Jean Schaefer explained the CJIS Security Architecture

DISC Security Services

Jim Logan discussed Netsonar, the security system that DISC uses. 

Closing

The meeting adjourned at 4:00 p.m.

The next meeting of the ITAB is scheduled for:

Wednesday, July 21, 1999, Docking State Office Building, Auditorium at 1:30 p.m.
