Meeting Minutes, December 1998
 

ITAB MEETING
Wednesday, December 16, 1998
534 S. Kansas Ave.
Top of the Tower
1:30 - 3:30 PM

John Oliver opened the meeting for Don Heiman. He discussed the Y2K Readiness Report. Hopefully it will be on the web by next week and you can update your reports. The website is at http://www.y2k.state.ks.us. Everyone can E-mail John to receive a signon and password. John asked that initially you request only one signon for entry and updates and one signon for read only.

January 10th is the date to get updates in to John before he runs the January report.

Gene James discussed Project Management Monitoring.

A draft copy of the proposed ITEC Project Management Methodology is now available on line for your review. To view the methodology you will need Word 97 or Word Viewer. Word Viewer can be found at http://www.microsoft.com/msdownload/

Rick Miller presented the Kansas Geospatial Data Addressing Standard and discussed addressing standards for the state. GIS standards in the past have become state policies and he suggested moving forward with this as a state policy. He requested feedback from the ITAB Committee to take back to the GIS Board in March. He also informed the ITAB Committee that there would be a 2-day event February 9 and 10 at the Historical Society. On February 9th there will be seminars on the Standards Form and on February 10th there will be exhibits and showcase demonstrations of GIS implementations.

Duncan Friend presented a Discussion Draft of proposed procedures for creating agency Internet domain names. The guideline presented is modeled after the State of Ohio's policy and will be discussed at the next ITAB meeting.

Don returned from Y2K Local Units of Government Summit in Hays and Wichita. Another meeting will be tomorrow in Topeka. The greatest need for Local units is for AS/400 testing platforms.

Don has submitted all project plans to the Joint Committee on Information Technology.

Local units of government are still working hard on Y2K. Don believes that they have a lower awareness of the importance of testing.

Don encouraged everyone to have a business contingency plan that would cover service delivery in the event of Y2K outage.

Dave Merrill discussed the progress of the technical architecture effort. The team is developing the next architecture component, and will try to come up with a draft of technical platform architecture by next meeting.

Mark Braun from the Department of Administration, Legal, talked about the acceptable use of the Internet. The Secretary of Administration wants employees to be able to use the Internet. Each agency sets their own standards for Internet use. We need to be consistent in our usage policies. Mark suggested that agencies discuss what they want and what kind of use is wrong or abusive.

Meeting adjourned at 3:30 p.m.

The next meeting of the ITAB is scheduled for:

Wednesday, January 20, 1999, Landon State Office Building, Room 106 at 1:30 PM.
