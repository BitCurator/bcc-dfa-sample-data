Meeting Minutes, February 1999

ITAB MEETING
Wednesday, February 17, 1999
Docking State Office Building
Auditorium
1:30 - 3:30 PM

CITO Briefings and Introductions 

Don Heiman went over the CITO report and reviewed some activities going on with the Legislature. Don announced that ITEC will be appointed by the week of February 15th. He hopes to have a list of the membership by the week of February 22nd.

Don stated that we are receiving a lot of hits on the Y2K web page, from as far away as Russia, Japan, United Arab Republic, and Europe. There were also a significant number of hits from News Services.

Don distributed a Summary of Y2K resources available to State agencies.

Don informed everyone that the Business Continuity Planning needed to be done by June 30th. Y2K Continuity Plans will be filed with Emergency Management.

Don reviewed the Executive Order that Governor Graves released on Y2K. All state agencies are to be in compliance with the reporting standards developed by the Kansas Information Technology office. He also discussed Y2K risk and explained the risk scale. The CITO points will trigger the risk score carding. A copy of State agency Y2K grades was requested by Senator Salisbury. The report was also requested by the Senate Ways and Means committee. The next committees to request it will be the Information Management committee and JCIT. Don has asked everyone to keep their Y2K reports current for the Legislature.

There are two bills to limit State Y2K liability. Don reviewed both bills. One bill limits consequential damages. The other bill grants sovereign immunity. The bill granting sovereign immunity was developed by the Kansas chamber of commerce. This bill has two key features: If vendors report compliance by September 1999 and go through notification procedures with their customers, they are allowed certain legal protections. The second part of the bill grants sovereign immunity to the State of Kansas and local units of governments.

The DISC data centers will be tested in SunGard’s Philadelphia data center on April 15. Technicians will attempt to recover portions of the state accounting system in the SunGard data center site. Don said DISC plans to idle its mainframes on December 31, 1999, at 11:45 PM to cross the millennium. The initiators will be down and no batch work will occur as we cross the millennium. The telecommunications networks provided by DISC will remain up.

John Oliver mentioned the requirement that agencies file their FY 2001 project plans by July 1, 1999.

IT Enterprise Architecture

Dave Merrill talked about Platform Architecture. Version 2.18 is ready for release. Dave said Version 3x should be ready by the week of March 1st. It should be available in two weeks. Ben Nelson discussed Network and Platform Architecture and distributed a handout.

Don would like everyone on ITAB to serve on at least one committee to review the Synthetic version of the Platform Architecture. A sign up sheet will be made available.

Project Management

John Oliver discussed Policy 2500, Project Status Reporting Policy. John also reviewed Policy 2510, Project Oversight Policy. The main goal is to put vendors on contract with the state to do external oversight for large projects. Oversight vendors will report to the agency and to the branch CITO. John talked about Project Management Policy which implements the project management methodology.

ITEC Policies

A motion was made to recommend six new ITEC policies. The motion was seconded and approved by consensus. The approved policies include:

Policy 2500 Project Reporting Policy 4000 Architecture Review Board

Policy 2510 Project Oversight Policy 4010 Architecture Compliance

Policy 2530 Project Management Policy 4020 Architecture Change

Telecommunications

Don talked about two Telecommunications activities: 1) AT&T will lower their long distance rates to DISC. Don will have Rate Reviews with AT&T every six months until March 1, 2001. 2) DISC has also created a committee customer council to review DISC interactive video rates.

Y2K Status

John Oliver asked that Y2K inventory data be up to date on the website. Status needs to be given and updated. A mainframe users group Y2K meeting was discussed. John would like to know if anyone would like to attend.

DISC is setting up a control center on the eighth floor of Landon for crossing the millennium. If overtime is needed for IT people, Don. will take care of it with Bill McGlasson. Don will request overtime for all IT classes from April 2000 through March 2001.

Web Page

Duncan Friend gave a presentation on the DofA web page.

Closing

The meeting adjourned at 3:30 p.m.

The next meeting of the ITAB is scheduled for:

Wednesday, March 17, 1999, Landon State Office Building, Room 106 at 1:30 p.m.
