Meeting Minutes, April 1999
 

ITAB MEETING
Friday, April 30, 1999
Nickell Memorial Armory
2722 SW Topeka Blvd.
1:30 - 3:30 PM

CITO Briefings

GroupWare: Don reported GroupWise 5.5 is Y2K ready. The Department of Administration will move to Microsoft Exchange because of compatibility and functionality issues with GroupWise and other systems in DofA. His main concern for GroupWise, Exchange, Lotus or any infrastructure product, is compatibility. He asked ITAB to thoroughly test infrastructure software upgrades with all third party dependent software.

Code Freeze: Federal Reserve and other Federal organizations are developing policies for Y2K code freeze. Don said ITAB should recommend to ITEC our code freeze policy. ITEC will meet July 13, Room 423-S in the State House from 1:00 – 3:00. Don feels agencies should closely manage vacation time for IT staff from November, 1999 through March 3, 2000, in order to handle problems that may arise.

The Federal Reserve has a stair-step policy, which includes a new development freeze policy beginning in July. They will start to do soft freezes in July, August and September. Those freezes firm up in October and November, and become solid in December, January and February.

Don asked members to update their Y2K status on the y2k.state.ks.us/agystatus Website. Soon Governor Graves will receive weekly Y2K reports on agency performance.

Workflow Committee Status Report

Sue Swartzman spoke about the Workflow Committee. Sue said her committee would schedule a half-day workshop on workflow.

Y2K Update

John Oliver mentioned two mailings that were sent out. One mailing asked agencies to update their Y2K asset lists. The other asked members to update all Y2K critical systems actual completion dates. John will send a reminder letter next week. John also stressed how important it is for agencies to give him accurate information.

Larry Kettlewell said the Federal and State Year 2000 Ready Data Exchange Website shows Kansas is 82% Y2K compliant. We are second in the nation for Y2K readiness.

Kansas Technical Architecture

Phil Wallack reviewed the purpose and structure of the Technical Architecture Review Board. ITAB members need to participate on at least one committee.

CITO Points

Don said agencies must have CITO points through testing in order to demonstrate due diligence. The CIO points indicate we are meeting our responsibilities for Y2K readiness.

ITS Consulting Services

Tim Blevins reviewed an ITS Consulting Services RFP. The RFP will help agencies contract IT services using a temporary programmer request task order process.

Gartner Group

John Carter discussed the various services provided by Gartner Group. John said he would be happy to come to agencies and demonstrate how to use Gartner services.
