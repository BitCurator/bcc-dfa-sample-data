Meeting Minutes, May 1999
 

ITAB MEETING

CITO Briefings

Don discussed the agency status of the mission critical applications and where we stand.  Don stated that it is imperative that we get as close to 100% as possible by June 30th.   There should be no mission critical application in a holding status. 

Don  went over the graded report that the governor receives.  There are 662 applications being  organized by state agencies and in the cabinet they are looking at these reports.  If you are sixty days behind in your schedule, you will drop a letter grade.   There are three small agencies that are not Y2K ready.  There existing application will not cross the millennium.  DISC has taken over responsibility for those agencies.  Twenty five percent of our exposure will be faced as we cross the July 1 fiscal year boundary.  Gartner group has also reported that eight to ten percent  of the exposure is crossing the millennium.  There is also a higher exposure crossing the leap year. Once we clear the fiscal year,  all the grades will return to A’s.  Don talked about doing health checks on agencies.  He will be sending letters to those agency heads.  Auditors will spend about three days with those agencies and grade them.  This will not happen until after July 1st. 

Don briefed ITAB on the outreach programs.  Outreach is done about every two months.  The next one is June 21st through June 23rd.  This outreach will be on Business Contingency Planning.  Agency heads have been invited.

The draft of the moratorium on code changes was discussed.  He would like everyone to spend about two or three days going over this draft and then send suggestions or comments to Phil Wallack.  Don would like to have a document in front of ITEC for the July meeting and have them endorse the moratorium.   Don went over the guidelines of the document, which is under the control of the agencies.  He would like you to go by these guidelines but he cannot mandate it. 

Year 2000 Update

John Oliver  handed out the summary of the quarterly project progress reports and went over the changes in it.  Don has not filed the quarterly report with the legislature for this cycle.  He is not getting enough information that he is proud enough to report.  Certain agencies reports are not complete. 

Technical Architecture:  Committee Update

Phil Wallack updated the makeup of the Technical Architecture committee.  A handout was dispersed and a formal letter will be going out thanking those who volunteered.  Let Phil know if you have  any other nominations.  

ITAB/ITEC Web Pages

Phil Wallack went over the contents of the web page and he would like any feed back on it.  Addresses for the web sites are: ITAB-http://da.state.ks.us/itab and for ITEC-http://da.state.ks.us/itec.  Please send Phil updates or changes on your e-mail addresses so he can place them in his mailing list and also change them on the web site. 

Don gave the time line for the architecture.  We want to be able to brief ITEC at the September meeting and call for conditional acceptance. The goal is to have all the architecture cleared through the committees by August.  They will call for formal acceptance in the December meeting.  Don wants all to make sure the content in each of those sections is an acceptable content. 

Firewall Options for State Agencies

Dave Timpany discussed options for firewall safety, what options and consequences there are for state agencies and then he went over the steps of safety for the network. 

Closing

Don changed the date for the next ITAB meeting.  It has been rescheduled to June 30th. 
Don has the JCIT schedule and they are going to start their interim hearings about July 15th. There is a virtual time machine being put into place for taxation systems and for the sharp systems. 

There are three things that Don wants done:

 1)      Go out to the intranet site and look at your assets for Y2K and get an upbeat report to Governor Graves.  
2)      Convene Technical Architecture committee and have one meeting to go over the Technical Architecture.  
3)      Don wants progress reports from all agencies.

The meeting adjourned at 2:40 p.m.


The next meeting of the ITAB is scheduled for:
Wednesday, June 30, 1999, Emergency Management, Room 11 at 1:30 p.m.
