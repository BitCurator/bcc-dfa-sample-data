Meeting Minutes, January 1999
 

ITAB MEETING
Wednesday, January 20, 1999
Landon State Office Building
Room 106
1:30 - 4:30 PM

CITO Briefings and Introductions

Don Heiman distributed the KITO 1999 Annual Report. He discussed House Bill 2018 which talks about each agency having a different file id key. Don said, "We are writing a fiscal note to this bill to try to estimate the cost for implementation." Don also asked ITAB members to review this bill and determine what impact the bill might have on their organization. He will include the cost information in his fiscal note to the bill.

Fred Boesch will be working with the legislature on a temporary basis. He can be reached by fax at 800-356-9712 or by phone at 352-243-1559.

Don said that the associated press will view State Y2K websites and use information from the websites to score state performance on Y2K.

Don discussed the Y2K grades reported on the website. The quality of agencies' Y2K monthly reports is very important. The reports contain schedule dates which are compared to the CITO schedule. Grades are based on the comparison. Don discussed how the grades are given. He also discussed CITO points (a total of 8 points are possible.) and how agencies receive them. Agencies should also consider Y2K backup plans in the event that their systems fail.

Don asked agencies to confirm the information on the Y2K website about their agency. Please return the letters you receive from John Oliver asking for confirmation that we have properly captured your information.

Y2K Status

John Oliver presented the Y2K Functional Report and said that he hopes to have the report ready on the web soon. He discussed the average grade for agency custom software.

Y2K Federal Interfaces

Larry Kettlewell gave a status report on where we stand with the Federal government Y2K interfaces. GSA hosts websites for reporting the Y2K status of Federal interfaces. Larry said State and Federal agencies use different names for the same programs. He needs ITAB's help to match the State name (program) of an interface to the name used by the Federal government. He would like people in Washington D.C. to know how much work we are doing in Kansas on Federal interfaces. Unfortunately, Larry cannot report because he can't match the interface names. He said the Federal government plans to make the interface web information available to the press on March 1st. He will be contacting agencies to help him.

SAS Users Group and State Contract

Barbara Moore of SAS Institute, Inc. gave an overview of the new state 2 year contract that SAS has signed with the State of Kansas. There will be quarterly meetings to keep everyone updated on SAS. The first meeting will be February 16, in room 106 of the Landon State Office Building. Joe Hennes, DISC, is our SAS representative. Joe's phone number is 296-2724.

IT Enterprise Architecture

Dave Merrill of Hitachi discussed the Kansas Technical Architecture. The draft of platform architectures will be on the KITO website prior to our next ITAB meeting. Dave discussed the importance of keeping architectures current. He believes the architecture should be organic - adaptive to a changing environment.

INK Report

Deb Luling, INK, discussed Information Network of Kansas' eight year history and the goals of the public/private partnership of the INK consortium. The Kansas INK program is a model for many other states. She discussed INK services and electronic commerce programs. INK is the "electronic face" for the State.

Project Management

Gene James reported on three project management policies. The policies cover Project Status Reporting, Oversight of Information Technology, and Project Management Plans. He asked agencies to review these policies and give input and comments before the next meeting.

The Department of Administration, Personnel Services, will implement training for IT Project Management Methodology Certification. Mary Adkins, Personnel Services, handed out a training pamphlet that includes an application form to register for training. She discussed the training schedule, the selection process for the vendor who will do the training, and how the vendor contract will be set up. Don suggested that ITAB create a committee to review the vendor bid for this training. Steve Tallen, Mary Hoover and Ben Nelson agreed to serve on the sub-committee.

Y2K Management Audits

Don asked if everyone had a copy of the "Outreach to the New Millennium" which is our Y2K planning guide. The guide contains a checklist. He encouraged agencies to fill out the checklist before an agency requests an audit. It is available on the web and will be very helpful to your organization.

Darrell Johnson, CTA, discussed the Y2K Process Reviews/Audits. He explained the benefits of an audit and the objective evidence to look for. If you go through the checklist in the planning guide in advance, it will make the audit go much easier. He also explained that this is a "review" and not a "certification" that applications are Y2K compliant. The audit is optional for CITO points.

CITRIX Presentation

Martin Cooper, Citrix, gave a presentation on Thin Client Architecture.

Closing

The meeting adjourned at 4:30 p.m.

The next meeting of the ITAB is scheduled for:

Wednesday, February 17, 1999, Docking State Office Building, Auditorium, at 1:30 PM.
