Overview of the CTA Year 2000 Remediation Contract

In December of 1996 the State of Kansas Department of Administration entered into a contract for Year 2000 remediation with Computer Technology Associates (CTA) of Bethesda, Maryland.  CTA is one of the leading consulting firms dealing in century date change issues.  they have experience working not only with several state governments on Year 2000 (Y2K) problems, but with the federal government as well.

The contract that the Department of Administration put in place is not limited for use to just state agencies. Local units of government such as cities and counties may use CTA under the provisions of the umbrella agreement. The contract sets forth a procedure to be followed for any agency or local unit that desires CTA services.  The umbrella contract identifies general and administrative contractual issues along with pricing and other generic provisions.

Agencies or local units contract with CTA under a "task order" process.  A task order is bundled in three parts:

  • Preliminary Request - a written response by the agency/local unit to CTA asking that certain Y2K issues be examined
  • Preliminary Response - written response by CTA to the inquiring agency/local unit setting forth the problem and possible solutions, in the form of deliverables, to the Y2K problem
  • Detailed Needs Analysis - complete work plans, cost, time schedules and deliverables regarding the Y2K issue(s) covered in the first two parts of the task order.

The Department of Administration's Division of Information Systems and Communications (DISC) has been assigned the responsibility of administration for this contract procedure. If you have any questions on the procedure for using this contract, please use the contact information listed above.
