Business Contingency Planning

What is a "Disaster"?

This may seem like a simple question, but, for the purposes of Business Contingency Planning the definition is "Any condition that prevents you from performing your critical business functions in an acceptable period of time."

Why do you need a Business Recovery Plan for the Year 2000 (Y2K) Problem?

Despite your best efforts, there is the potential that some of your critical business functions may experience operational difficulties during the Y2K transition. Your clients and customers will expect you to have thought through the process enough to continue your most critical functions.

What is a Recovery Plan?

A Recovery Plan is the detailed procedures, resources and requirements you need to execute a successful recovery of your critical business functions. The plan must be tested to be complete.

What is contained in a Recovery Plan?

  • Names and telephone numbers
  • Roles and responsibilities defined
  • Escalation procedures documented
  • Relocation information
  • Offsite storage information
  • Vital records and data location
  • Critical vendor information
  • Media information to reassure the public that you are in control of the situation.

This information must be readily available outside of your current facility.

Business Contingency Planning Resources

The State of Kansas has the following business contingency resources available to state agencies and local governmental units:

  • The Department of Administration's Division of Information Systems and Communications (DISC) Business Contingency Planning pages
  • Kansas Information Resource Council (KIRC) Policy #3200
    Key concepts related to developing a business contingency plan
  • KIRC Policy #3210
    Outlines the process and contents of a business contingency plan for information resources.
  • Model Plan(updated 3/3/99)
    Provides a sample plan.

State Contract for Business Contingency Planning and Other Related Resources

The State has a contract with SunGard to provide assistance to state agencies and local governmental units as they develop their business contingency plans. The process includes the development of a task order for the services and deliverables that SunGard negotiates with the local governmental organization. Contact the State of Kansas Year 2000 Awareness Center at (785) 368-8300 for more information.
