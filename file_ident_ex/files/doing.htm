What Kansas State Government is Doing About the Year 2000 Problem

Our commitment

There have been numerous gloomy scenarios painted about the country's ability to cope with the Year 2000 (Y2K) problem-- and probably most are overstated. Nevertheless, Kansas State Government takes very seriously the potential for disruptions and inconvenience caused by this problem. Emphasis on continued delivery of basic services like water, electricity, emergency services and the ability to conduct business on behalf of the citizens of Kansas is our number one priority. State Government agencies are continuing to execute their plans of action to remediate Year 2000 problems. Included as an integral part of this program is business contingency and disaster recovery planning which will allow an orderly, fully functional government in the Year 2000 and beyond. We believe these steps will minimize, if not curtail altogether, the effects of the Year 2000 problem.

What we're doing

The Department of Administration's Division of Information Systems and Communications (DISC) is the designated organization for coordinating and reporting on the State's Year 2000 efforts  (they are also responsible for this web site). The Director of DISC also serves as a Chief Information Technology Officer (CITO) and reports directly to the Governor.

The State began its efforts on the Year 2000 problem in early 1996. Since that time state government has devoted over 172,000 hours to assessment, repair and testing of hardware and software for Year 2000 problems. With about 98,000 computers and computer-related devices across state government the task has not been a small endeavor. Links to information on the status of the State's Year 2000 readiness can be found on the main page of this site.   The status of interfaces with the Federal government can be found under Federal Government Interfaces.

DISC has now completed assessments of what needs to be done and is in the various stages of aggressively continuing to implement plans for repairs, testing and user acceptance in various State agencies. DISC will conclude these efforts in most cases with formal audit confirmation. The underlying goal throughout all of this effort is to allow Kansas State government to continue delivery of goods and services without interruption.

DISC has also launched an outreach program. Entitled "Outreach to the New Millennium", it is intended to provide information for both local governments and private sector business.
